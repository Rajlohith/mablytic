Daily 30-Minute Walk Reduces Cardiovascular Disease Risk by 40%, New Study Confirms

By Dr. James Patel · Health & Science Editor

A landmark 12-year study tracking 85,000 participants across 14 countries has confirmed that adults who walk briskly for 30 minutes a day, five days a week, reduce their risk of cardiovascular disease by 40% compared to sedentary peers. The research, published in the New England Journal of Medicine, also found significant reductions in type-2 diabetes risk (31%) and all-cause mortality (22%). Lead researcher Dr. Aisha Morales from Johns Hopkins noted that even short 10-minute walking sessions spread throughout the day produced meaningful benefits, making the prescription highly accessible. Wearable devices played a key role in the study's data collection, with participants using smartwatches to log activity automatically. Public health officials say the findings reinforce walking as the single most cost-effective public health intervention available.
